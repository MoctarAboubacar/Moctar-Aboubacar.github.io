About

Background

I am Lead Economist for Eastern and Southern Africa at the World Food Programme, based in Nairobi. My work centres on food security evidence — supporting WFP country offices across 20 countries with assessments, analysis, and the data products that inform humanitarian response.

My methods range from household survey analysis and IPC food security classifications to geospatial vulnerability mapping and causal inference techniques. I work primarily in R and Python, and I believe rigorous, reproducible analysis is the foundation of effective humanitarian action.
